As Arvin and I have both traveled to many places and have been in many different situations while travelling, we thought we should share some tips and tricks on what we think could benefit a new traveler.

1. Budgeting Tips
  • Book flights early: The later you book your flight, the more expensive it will be.
  • Use public transportation: After landing, consider taking public transportation or walking to save money and explore.
  • Book accommodations early: Look for places to stay when you book your flight to get better deals.
  • Eat local: Try local markets and street food to save money and experience authentic cuisine.
  • Find free activities: Most cities have free or low-cost activities to help you explore.
  • Pack essentials: Bring all your essentials to avoid buying them in a different currency at higher prices.
2. Packing Essentials
  • Backpack: Lightweight and comfortable backpack for daily use.
  • Travel Documents: Passport, visas, insurance, and copies stored securely.
  • Clothing: Lightweight, quick-drying clothes that can be layered.
  • Reusable Water Bottle: Stay hydrated while reducing plastic waste.
  • Multi-Purpose Shoes: Comfortable shoes suitable for various activities.
  • First Aid Kit: Basic medical supplies for minor emergencies.
3. Cheap Destinations

Top three budget-friendly places to travel to:

  • Vietnam: Affordable accommodations ($5-$15/night), delicious street food ($1-$3/meal), and inexpensive transportation.
  • Bolivia: One of South America's most budget-friendly countries with local meals ($2-$5) and accommodations under $20/night.
  • Cambodia: Stunning landscapes, historic sites, amazing street food ($3/meal), and hotels under $20/night.
