As Arvin and I have both traveled to many places and have been in many different situations while travelling. We thought we
should share some tips and tricks on what we think could benefit a new traveler.

1. Budgeting Tips:
  • When traveling the most important step is to book your flight early. The later you book your flight the more expensive
    it will be.
  • After landing it is important to consider taking public transportation, even better is walking to not only save money but
    also explore the city on your walk.
  • When looking for places to stay, try to consider this on the same day you book your flight, the sooner it is booked the
    cheaper, and also gives you plenty of time to look through the deals and find the best one that fits your budget.
  • Try to eat at local markets and street food stalls because this will not just save you money, but you will experience the
    true life in that city.
  • Try to find low-cost activities. Most cities have plenty of free or low-cost activities that you can do to help you explore
    the city more while being on a budget.
  • Most importantly, pack all your essentials so that you're not buying them in a different currency. You might often be
    tempted to buy sunscreen when you arrive in that city because you don't want to pack it, but sunscreen in that city might
    cost you three times more than what you would have paid back home.
2. Packing Essentials:
  • Backpack: Make sure you have a lightweight and comfortable backpack that you can use to pack your stuff on your trips around the city.
  • Travel Documents: There is not much to say about this as you can't travel without them, and without these you will face many challenges.
  • Clothing: Make sure you have enough clothes for the duration of your travel. Pack lightweight, quick-drying clothes that can be layered to make sure they can all fit in your suitcase.
  • Reusable Water Bottle: When you travel to a new city you will be out and exploring a lot so you need to make sure you are staying healthy and hydrated.
  • Multi-Purpose Shoes: Make sure you have at least one pair of comfortable shoes that can be used for different activities. This will save a lot of space when packing for a trip and lower the weight you'll be carrying.
Cheap Destinations to travel to:

Top three budget-friendly places to travel to:

  • Vietnam: A very affordable country with accommodations, transportation, and affordable food. Street food meals like pho only cost $1-$3 and are super delicious. The hotels are very affordable and range from $5-$15 per night, and the more people you are staying with, the less you are paying.
  • Bolivia: One of the most budget-friendly countries in all of South America with local meals ranging from $2-$5. Very cheap traveling and places to stay at for under $20 a night.
  • Cambodia: A very historic country in Southeast Asia with stunning landscapes. Cambodia has amazing street food that costs no more than $3 per meal. Plenty of cheap hotels to stay at for a night for under $20.
